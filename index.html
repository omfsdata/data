Interview dates shown are from the 2026 application cycle (last year), compiled from a publicly shared applicant spreadsheet — 2027-cycle dates are typically not released until interview invitations go out in the fall. Verify all dates directly with each program.

  • Source: AAOMS "Accredited Advanced Training Programs in Oral and Maxillofacial Surgery" directory (federal + state programs).
  • Every non-military program's own website was checked against the directory's "Program Type" field. This surfaced ~20 programs that actually run parallel 4-year (certificate-only) and 6-year (MD-integrated) tracks side by side even though the directory lists them under a single "Dual Degree" label — these are shown here as Both (e.g. Maryland, Emory, UF Gainesville, NYU, UConn, UMKC, Rutgers, Boston University, USC, Loma Linda, UAB, UIC, LSU New Orleans & Shreveport, Geisinger, Rochester Eastman, Northwell/LIJ, Vanderbilt, UT Houston, UT Knoxville, and Pittsburgh).
  • One program — Cook County Health — was listed as "Single Degree" in the directory but actually runs a 4-year/6-year dual-track split.
  • A few programs (Texas A&M, Case Western, UT San Antonio, UT Memphis) run a secondary track that's either restricted (military- or international-applicants-only) or offered only occasionally; these keep their primary directory label with a note.
  • Nova Southeastern is mid-transition from a 4-year to a 6-year program.
  • Montefiore was confirmed as Single Degree (the directory left this field blank). All other programs matched their directory listing on direct verification.
  • Locations are approximate (city/campus level). Puerto Rico's program is shown in the inset box since it falls outside the continental projection.
